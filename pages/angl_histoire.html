History of Deezer

Deezer Corporate Foundation

In August 2007, Deezer was founded by two French entrepreneurs, Daniel Marhely and Jonathan Benassaya. They had the idea of creating a music streaming platform that would enable people to listen to music on the Internet, legally and for free. music on the Internet, legally and for free. This idea laid the foundations for what was to become Deezer.

Signing contracts with labels

On October 10, 2007, following an agreement with Sony BMG, Deezer offered a further 165,000 tracks, then from January 10, 2008 and the signing with Believe and Because, the total was one and a half million tracks available.

Signing contracts with labels such as Sony BMG, Believe and Because has enabled Deezer to establish itself as a leading music streaming platform.

Deezer goes global

Deezer launches its international service and becomes one of the leading players in global music streaming.

Thanks to its globalization efforts, Deezer has been able to offer its platform to a worldwide audience, enabling music lovers all over the world to discover and enjoy a vast library of songs from different genres and cultures.

Flow functionality

Deezer introduces Flow, a personalized music experience.

With "Flow", Deezer users can discover new songs, artists and albums based on their musical preferences. The feature analyzes the user's tastes and generates personalized playlists, offering a unique music-listening experience tailored to each individual.
